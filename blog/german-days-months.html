German Days & Months:
Weekdays, 12 Months, Seasons

From Montag to Sonntag, Januar to Dezember — a comprehensive guide with date and time expressions.

📚 Reading time: ~6 minutes · A1 level

Knowing German days, months and seasons is useful in countless situations — from making appointments to saying dates. The good news: German month names are very similar to English, so they are easy to pick up.

Grammar note: Days of the week and months all use the article der in German (der Montag, der Januar). Seasons also use der (der Sommer).

Memory tip: Mittwoch literally means "middle of the week" (Mitte = middle, Woche = week). Many German day names hide the word "Tag" (day): Mon-tag, Diens-tag…

Sentences with Days of the Week

GermanEnglish
Am Montag habe ich Schule.I have school on Monday.
Wir treffen uns am Freitag.We are meeting on Friday.
Am Wochenende schlafe ich lange.I sleep in on the weekend.
Jeden Mittwoch gehe ich ins Fitnessstudio.Every Wednesday I go to the gym.

⚠️ Watch out: When saying a day, the preposition "an" is used and contracts to am: an dem Montag → am Montag.

Sentences with Months

GermanEnglish
Im Januar ist es sehr kalt.It is very cold in January.
Mein Geburtstag ist im März.My birthday is in March.
Wir fahren im August in Urlaub.We go on holiday in August.
Der Dezember ist mein Lieblingsmonat.December is my favourite month.

Rule: When naming a month, use im (= in dem): im Januar, im Sommer.

Saying Dates

In German, dates are expressed with ordinal numbers. Examples:

  • der erste Januar → January 1st (written: der 1. Januar)
  • der vierzehnte Juni → June 14th
  • Heute ist der zwanzigste März. → Today is March 20th.
  • Ich bin am dritten Mai geboren. → I was born on May 3rd.
