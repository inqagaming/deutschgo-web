Goethe A1 & A2:
How to Pass the German Exam

Format breakdown, section-by-section strategy and an 8-week study plan to pass your Goethe-Institut exam with confidence.

📋 Reading time: ~9 minutes · A1–A2 level

The Goethe-Zertifikat A1 and A2 are internationally recognised German language certificates issued by the Goethe-Institut. They are accepted for visa applications, job positions and academic programmes worldwide. With the right preparation strategy, passing is absolutely achievable.

Exam Format

SectionDurationPointsWhat it tests
Hören (Listening)~20 min25 ptsShort dialogues, announcements, announcements
Lesen (Reading)~25 min25 ptsNotices, messages, short texts
Schreiben (Writing)~20 min25 ptsForm completion + short message/email
Sprechen (Speaking)~15 min25 ptsIntroduce yourself + ask/answer questions

Pass mark: You need at least 60 points out of 100 overall, AND at least 60% in each individual section. You cannot fail one section and pass with the others.

Section-by-Section Strategies

Hören (Listening)

  • Read the questions before the audio plays
  • Focus on key words — you don't need to understand every word
  • Practise with DW Deutsch and Slow German podcasts at A1/A2 speed

Lesen (Reading)

  • Scan the text for the main idea first, then read questions
  • Look for signal words: nicht (not), nur (only), immer (always)
  • Don't spend more than 4–5 minutes on one text

Schreiben (Writing)

  • Learn the standard letter/email greeting and closing formulas
  • Keep sentences short and clear — examiner rewards completeness, not complexity
  • Always answer all bullet points asked in the question

Sprechen (Speaking)

  • Prepare a 60-second self-introduction (name, origin, job, hobbies)
  • Use connectors: und, aber, weil, deshalb
  • If you don't understand: "Können Sie das bitte wiederholen?" = "Can you repeat that please?"

8-Week Preparation Plan

  • 1

    Week 1: Self-Assessment

    Take a free A1/A2 practice test online. Identify your weak sections.

  • 2

    Week 2: Vocabulary Foundation

    Focus on A1 topic vocabulary: personal info, home, work, time, food.

  • 3

    Week 3: Grammar Essentials

    Articles, Nominativ/Akkusativ, basic verb conjugation (Präsens + Perfekt).

  • 4

    Week 4: Listening Practice

    15 minutes of A1-level audio daily. DW "Deutsch lernen" is excellent.

  • 5

    Week 5: Writing Practice

    Write 5 short emails or form responses. Get feedback from an AI tutor.

  • 6

    Week 6: Speaking Practice

    Practice your self-introduction daily. Use DeutschGo AI conversations.

  • 7

    Week 7: Mock Exams

    Complete 2 full mock exams under timed conditions. Review every mistake.

  • 8

    Week 8: Light Review

    Only review known material. Rest well. Do not try to learn new content.

Exam Day Tips

⏰

Arrive Early

Arrive 15–20 minutes before the exam. Bring your ID and registration confirmation.

📝

Read Questions First

In reading and listening, always read the questions before the texts or audio.

🗣️

Keep Speaking

In the speaking section, if you make a mistake, keep going — don't stop and restart.

✅

Check Your Answers

Use remaining time to review. Watch for double negatives and tricky wording.
