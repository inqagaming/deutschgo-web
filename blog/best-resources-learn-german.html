Best Resources for
Learning German in 2026

Apps, YouTube channels and podcasts that actually work — curated and organized by level.

📱 Reading time: ~7 minutes · All levels

With so many options available, it's hard to know where to focus. Here's a curated list of the most effective German learning resources in 2026 — tested and organized so you can build a complete learning routine.

The best approach: Combine one structured learning app (for vocabulary + grammar), one YouTube channel (for listening + culture) and one podcast (for ear training). That's the full trifecta.

📱 Mobile Apps

🇩🇪
App · A1–B2
DeutschGo
Structured vocabulary with spaced repetition, gender-colored flashcards, grammar mini-games and AI conversation practice. Ideal for learners who want a complete, focused German learning experience.
🦉
App · A1–B1
Duolingo
Game-like exercises great for building habits and beginner vocabulary. Best used as a supplement rather than a primary resource — the gamification helps consistency but depth is limited.
🃏
App · All levels
Anki
Open-source spaced repetition flashcard system. Steep initial learning curve but extremely powerful for vocabulary retention. Download pre-made German decks or create your own.

▶️ YouTube Channels

🎓
YouTube · A1–B2
Deutsch für Euch
Katerina explains German grammar clearly in English. One of the best channels for understanding the "why" behind German grammar rules. Great for intermediate learners.
📰
YouTube · B1–C1
DW Deutsch lernen
Deutsche Welle's official learning channel. News in simple German, slow-spoken news (Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten), and structured courses. Excellent for intermediate learners building listening skills.
🛣️
YouTube · B1–C1
Easy German
Street interviews with native speakers, subtitled in both German and English. Superb for real-world, colloquial German. The community and Patreon content are excellent extras.

🎧 Podcasts

☕
Podcast · A2–B2
Coffee Break German
Radio-Lingua's Coffee Break German is structured like a course — each episode builds on the last. Excellent production quality and clear explanations make it perfect for independent learners.
🐢
Podcast · A2–B1
Slow German
Short episodes (5–10 min) spoken slowly on everyday German topics. Great for ear training at the A2–B1 transition. Transcripts available on the website.
📻
Podcast · B1+
DW — Deutsch Warum Nicht?
A classic beginner-to-intermediate series from Deutsche Welle. Follow Andreas through everyday German life situations. Free online with transcripts and exercises.
